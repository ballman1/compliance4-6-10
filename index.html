New York State Requirements

EpiAccess helps you maintain compliance with NY State Public Health Law § 3000-c requirements:

  • Maintain minimum of 2 epinephrine auto-injectors on premises
  • Have a minimum of one trained staff member
  • Perform monthly inventory and training verification
  • Store devices properly and maintain accurate records

Compliance Checklist

Good Samaritan Protection

Under NY Public Health Law § 3000-b, individuals who reasonably and in good faith administer epinephrine to someone experiencing anaphylaxis are protected from liability.

Requirements for protection:

  1. Act in good faith as an ordinary prudent person would
  2. Have completed an approved training program
  3. Immediately call 911 after administration

Epinephrine Auto-Injector Inventory

NY State Requirements

Under NY Public Health Law § 3000-c, businesses may maintain epinephrine auto-injectors for emergency use. A minimum of 2 devices must be maintained on premises, stored properly and checked monthly for expiration dates.

Compliance Center

NY State Compliance Requirements

To maintain compliance with New York State regulations, businesses must:

  1. Maintain at least two epinephrine auto-injectors on premises
  2. Ensure all devices are not expired and properly stored
  3. Have a minimum of one trained staff member
  4. Perform monthly inventory checks
  5. Maintain accurate records of training and inventory
